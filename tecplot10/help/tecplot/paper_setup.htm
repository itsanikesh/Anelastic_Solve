Paper Setup

Use the Paper Setup dialog to specify the size, orientation, and screen fill color of your Tecplot paper. You use the paper to position frames and other plot elements in the exact position you want them printed.

 

The following options are available:

  • Size: Select the paper size. The dimensions of the chosen paper size are shown in the Dimensions text field. The units shown in that field are determined by the Spacing option in the Ruler/Grid dialog.

  • Choose from one of the following paper sizes option buttons:

  • Letter: Standard U.S. Letter size, 8.5 by 11 inches.

  • Double: Ledger size, 11 by 17 inches.

  • A4: A4 size paper, 29.7 by 21 centimeters.

  • A3: A3 size paper, 42 by 29.7 centimeters.

  • Custom 1: Custom size of 8.5 by 14 inches.

  • Custom 2: Custom size of 8 by 10 inches.

  • If you choose a paper size that your current printer does not support, Tecplot tells you and asks you to choose a different paper size. The paper size can also be modified in the Print Setup dialog. If you make a change in one dialog, Tecplot immediately reflects the change in the other dialog.

  • Orientation: Choose the paper orientation from the following option button options:

  • Portrait: Portrait orientation, that is, the long side of the paper vertical.

  • Landscape: Landscape orientation, that is, the long side of the paper horizontal.

  • Orientation can also be modified in the Print Setup dialog. If you make a change in one dialog, Tecplot immediately reflects the change in the other dialog.

  • Dimensions (Display only): This area displays the dimensions of the selected paper size, in units determined by the Ruler Spacing option in the Ruler/Grid dialog.

  • Show Paper on Screen: Select this check box to display the paper in the Tecplot workspace.

  • Use Paper Fill Color when Printing: Select this check box to have your printer flood your plotting paper with the specified page fill color.

  • Paper Fill Color: Use this drop-down to choose a color from the Select Color dialog to use as the fill color on the page when printing.
