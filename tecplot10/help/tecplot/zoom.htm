Zoom

Zoom into or away from the plot. There are two zoom modes – axis zooming and paper zooming.

 

To zoom:

Drag the magnifying glass cursor to draw a box about the region that you want to fit into the frame. The region within the view box will be resized to fit into the frame. If Snap to Grid is selected, you cannot make the zoom box larger than the grid area.

 

To return to the previous view:

Choose Last (Ctrl-L) from the View menu. To restore the original 2-D view, choose Fit to Full Size (Ctrl-F).

 

To zoom 2- and 3-D axes:

  • 2D Independent Axis Mode: Allows the selected region to expand to exactly fit in the frame. The axes are rescaled independently to fit the zoom box.

  • 2D Dependent Axis Mode: Results in the plot being resized to fit the longest dimension of the zoom box to the frame (since the relative axis scale is fixed).

  • 3D Axis Mode: Only changes the size (or magnification) of the data and axes projected onto the frame. The plot is resized such that the longest dimension of the zoom box fits into the frame.

 

To zoom the paper:

Shift-drag the magnifying glass cursor to draw a box about the region that you want to magnify. The plot is resized such that the longest dimension of the zoom box fits into the workspace. You can fit one or all frames to the workspace by using the Fit Selected Frames to Workspace or the Fit All Frames to Workspace options of the Workspace menu. Both options are alternative methods of zooming the paper.

 

To return to the default paper view:

Choose Fit Paper to Workspace from the Workspace menu.
