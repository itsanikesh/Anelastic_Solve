Extract Slice from Plane

Tecplot allows you to slice one or more zones in 3D Cartesian mode with a planar surface called the cutting plane. One or more triangle or quadrilateral elements are generated where the cutting plane intersects a cell (or element) in the 3-D zone(s). These elements are combined into two new FE-surface zones called slice zones.

 

To extract a slice, click Extract after you have positioned the cutting plane.

 

The following options are available:

 

Slice Plane

  • Arbitrary: An arbitrary cutting plane. You may specify the position and orientation of the cutting plane by entering either the coordinates of three points which define the plane or the origin and normal vector for the plane, or you can use the Position and Rotate About regions to interactively place and rotate the cutting plane.

  • Constant X: A cutting plane of constant X-value. Specify the X-value with in the Position region.

  • Constant Y: As Constant X above, but for a cutting plane of constant Y-value.

  • Constant Z: As Constant X above, but for a cutting plane of constant Z-value.

  • Position

  • X: Enter the exact coordinate for the X-coordinate of the origin of the cutting plane or use the slider to specify the coordinate as a percentage of the X-range.

  • Y: Enter the exact coordinate for the Y-coordinate of the origin of the cutting plane, or use the slider to specify the coordinate as a percentage of the Y-range.

  • Z: Enter the exact coordinate for the Z-coordinate of the origin of the cutting plane. or use the slider to specify the coordinate as a percentage of the Z-range.

 

Define Arbitrary Plane by Entering

  • Three Points: Calls up the Enter Three Points dialog, which you use to specify the cutting plane by entering the X-, Y-, and Z-coordinates of three points on the cutting plane (nine numbers in all). These points must form a triangle; they cannot be coincident or collinear.

  • Origin and Normal: Calls up the Enter Slice Origin and Normal dialog, which you use to specify the cutting plane by entering the coordinates of a point and the components of a vector normal. Using this option, you enter six numbers to specify the cutting plane. First, you are asked for the X-, Y-, and Z-coordinates of a point on the cutting plane (called the slice origin). Next, you are asked for the X-, Y-, and Z-components of a vector normal to the cutting plane (called the slice normal).

 

Rotate About

  • X-Axis: Click the increase button to rotate the cutting plane clockwise about the X-axis; click the decrease button to rotate the plane counter-clockwise.

  • Y-Axis: Click the increase button to rotate the cutting plane clockwise about the Y-axis; click the decrease button to rotate the plane counter-clockwise.

  • Z-Axis: Click the increase button to rotate the cutting plane clockwise about the Z-axis; click the decrease button to rotate the plane counter-clockwise.

  • Step Size (deg): Specify the step size for the rotation, in degrees; each click of the mouse on one of the increase or decrease buttons corresponds to a rotation of Step Size.

 

You can either enter an angle in the text field, or choose one of the preset options from the drop-down. The default values available are: Small=1, Medium=5, and Large=15. You can alter these default values from the Size Preferences dialog.

  • Show Trace: Select this option to see the position of the current slice.

  • Force Extraction to a Single Zone: Select this option to combine all elements created by slicing the 3-D zones into a single zone. If this option is not selected, each source zone and each topologically independent region of the slice creates a new zone.

  • Create Slice From: Select this option to extract a slice from volume zones, surface zones, surfaces of volume zones, or linear zones.
