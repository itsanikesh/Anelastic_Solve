Push Current Frame Back

Pushes the current frame to the bottom of the stack, making the next frame current.

 

For example, suppose you have three frames, frame 1 with a red header, frame 2 with a green header, and frame 3 with a blue header. Furthermore, suppose that the initial configuration of the frame stack is as follows:

  • Frame 1 (current/top of stack).

  • Frame 2 (middle of stack).

  • Frame 3 (bottom of stack).

 

When you select Push Back from the Frame menu, frame 1 moves to the bottom of the stack, and frame 2 is made the current frame. The stack now looks like:

  • Frame 2 (current/top of stack).

  • Frame 3 (middle of stack).

  • Frame 1 (bottom of stack).
